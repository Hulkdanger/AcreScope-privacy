AcreAlert Privacy Policy

Effective May 26, 2026

AcreAlert is a mobile app that shows locust activity from public data feeds and community sightings, and gives growers a location-aware risk score. This Privacy Policy explains what data the app collects, why, who processes it on our behalf, and what choices you have. It applies to the AcreAlert iOS app and, where present, its web version.

The app is operated by Yuvraj Dhamija, an individual developer (the “developer”, “we”, “us”). For the purposes of the EU/UK GDPR and similar laws, the developer is the data controller.

Summary

  • What we collect: approximate and precise location, a per-install identifier and push token, the structured fields of any community sightings you submit, crash and performance diagnostics, and your email if you choose to contact us.
  • Why: to display nearby locust observations, compute your personalized risk score, send push alerts for swarms in your area, debug the app, and respond to your messages.
  • Where it goes: Google Firebase / Google Cloud Platform (United States) is our backend and crash/performance provider. Apple Push Notification Service delivers iOS pushes. Google Maps powers the map display. The full list is in “Service providers” below.
  • What we do not do: we do not sell or rent your data. We do not use it for advertising or cross-app tracking. We do not collect the IDFA/Advertising Identifier. We do not run behavioural analytics (no Firebase Analytics, Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Segment, Facebook SDK, etc.). The only Google tools we use are Crashlytics and Performance Monitoring for crash and performance debugging, and Firebase backend services described below. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.
  • Your controls: you can revoke location permission, disable push notifications, request deletion of your data, and stop using the app at any time.

2. What we do not collect

We want to be specific about this, because many apps in this category do collect these and we don’t.

  • We do not collect the iOS IDFA (Advertising Identifier) or Android AAID. The App Tracking Transparency framework is not used because the app does not track you for advertising.
  • We do not use behavioural analytics tools such as Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics (the specific Google product that tracks user events, screen views, funnels, and conversions), Sentry, AppsFlyer, Branch, Adjust, Amplitude, Mixpanel, Segment, OneSignal, Braze, Datadog, Kochava, Singular, FullStory, Hotjar, PostHog, or any Meta / Facebook SDK. We do use Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Performance Monitoring — these are separate Firebase products that collect crash reports and performance traces (not behaviour) and are described in Section 6 below.
  • We do not display third-party advertising and do not integrate any ad network (no AdMob, Google Ad Manager, etc.).
  • We do not collect your name, phone number, contacts, photos, microphone audio, health data, financial data, browsing history, or search history.
  • We do not require account creation, and we do not link your data to a real-world identity unless you choose to email us.

3. Location data — how it is used

Location is the core input to the app. When you grant the permission, your device’s operating system (Apple Core Location on iOS, Google Play Services Fused Location on Android, or the browser Geolocation API on the web) returns latitude / longitude to AcreAlert.

  • On-device use: we use your location to centre the map, sort observations by distance, and compute your risk score.
  • Backend use: if you enable push notifications, the app sends your latitude, longitude, and notification radius to our registerPushDevice Cloud Function (running on Google Cloud Platform in us-central1), together with your Installations ID and FCM token, so that we can send you push alerts when a relevant locust observation appears near you.
  • Map tiles: our risk-map tiles are served by our own tile server (Martin, running on Google Cloud Run, region us-central1). Tile requests carry your IP address and the tile coordinates you are looking at, but no Installations ID, FCM token, or sighting content.
  • Sighting submissions: when you submit a community sighting, the location you select for that sighting is stored alongside the structured fields of the submission. This is the point of the feature.

You can revoke location permission at any time in your device settings (iOS: Settings > Privacy & Security > Location Services > AcreAlert). If you revoke it, the map will still load, but the risk score, “sort by distance,” and nearby-push features will not work.

4. Push notifications

If you allow notifications, Firebase Cloud Messaging issues a push token for your installation. We store that token together with your most recent reported location and chosen notification radius, so our backend can decide whether to push you an alert about a swarm. Apple Push Notification Service relays the message to your iOS device; Google Mobile Services does the equivalent on Android.

You can turn off notifications at any time in your device settings or in-app. If you turn them off, we stop sending pushes; the token and last-known location used for push routing are deleted on your next interaction with the app or on your request.

5. Community sightings (user-generated content)

AcreAlert lets you submit a sighting using a set of structured fields. There are no free-text fields and no photo uploads. Each submission is stored with the Installations ID of the submitter, is reviewed by us before it appears on the public map, and is visible to other users with the location and structured fields you selected (but not with any identifier that ties it to you).

You can report another user’s sighting in-app. We may remove a sighting and/or restrict an installation from submitting further sightings if it violates these terms or applicable law. This complies with Apple’s App Review Guideline 1.2 for user-generated content.

6. Diagnostics and abuse-prevention

The app uses Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Performance Monitoring to collect crash reports and performance traces. These are linked to a Firebase Installations ID, not to your name or email. The app uses Firebase App Check (backed by Apple App Attest / DeviceCheck on iOS and Google Play Integrity on Android, and Google reCAPTCHA v3 on the web) to verify that backend requests come from a genuine copy of the app. Apple, Google, and Google reCAPTCHA see hashed device or site signals as part of this attestation.

8. International data transfers

Our backend and all of the sub-processors above operate in the United States. If you use AcreAlert from outside the United States — including the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, Kenya, Nigeria, India, or anywhere else — your data is transferred to and processed in the United States.

For transfers from the EEA / UK, we rely on the Standard Contractual Clauses adopted by the European Commission (and the UK International Data Transfer Addendum where applicable), which our sub-processors (Google, Apple) make available to their customers. For transfers from Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, and similar jurisdictions, we rely on the equivalent transfer mechanisms permitted by local law.

9. How we share information

  • Service providers: as listed above, only to operate the service.
  • Other users: the structured fields and location of a community sighting you submit are displayed publicly in the app after review. No identifier that links the sighting to you is shown.
  • Legal: we may disclose data if we are legally required to (e.g. a valid court order, lawful government request) or to protect the rights, safety, and integrity of AcreAlert or its users.
  • No sale, no “sharing” for cross-context behavioural advertising as those terms are defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA / CPRA). We have not sold or shared your personal information in the previous 12 months, and we do not intend to.

10. Retention and deletion

  • Community sightings: retained while the app is in operation, because they are part of the public locust-observation map. You can request deletion of your specific submissions by emailing us.
  • Push registration (Installations ID, FCM token, last-known location, notification radius): retained while the app is installed and notifications are enabled, and deleted on request or when the registration is no longer used.
  • Crash and performance data: retained for the default Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Performance retention periods (currently 90 days for Crashlytics events, up to 90 days for Performance traces), then deleted by Google.
  • Support emails: retained as long as needed to resolve your request, and longer if required for legal record-keeping.

11. Your rights and choices

Everyone

  • Revoke location and notification permissions in your device settings at any time.
  • Uninstall the app at any time. Uninstalling stops further data collection by the app.
  • Email us at AcreAlertApp@gmail.com to request a copy or deletion of data we hold about you. Please include “AcreAlert Privacy” in the subject line.

European Economic Area, United Kingdom (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are in the EEA or the UK, you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict processing of, and port your personal data, and to object to processing. The legal bases on which we rely are: performance of a contract (operating the app you have installed), legitimate interests (security, abuse prevention, crash and performance debugging), and consent where required (location, push notifications). You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing. You may also lodge a complaint with your national data protection authority.

California (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to know what personal information we have collected, to request deletion, to correct inaccurate information, and to opt out of any “sale” or “sharing” (we do neither). Precise geolocation is treated as sensitive personal information under California law; we use it only to provide the service you requested, which is one of the purposes for which sensitive personal information may be used without a separate right-to-limit signal under CPRA. We do not discriminate against users who exercise these rights.

Kenya, Nigeria, South Africa, India, and other jurisdictions

Where local law (e.g. the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019, the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023, South Africa’s POPIA, India’s DPDP Act 2023) grants you rights similar to those above, we honour those rights. Contact us at the address below.

No system is 100% secure; if we become aware of a security incident affecting your data, we will notify affected users and regulators as required by law.
